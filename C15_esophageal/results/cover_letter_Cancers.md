We respectfully submit our manuscript titled **"Surgical Margin Status, CCRT, and Deep
Learning–Identified Patient Subtypes as Independent Prognostic Determinants in Esophageal
Cancer"** for consideration for publication in *Cancers*. This work has not been published
previously, is not under consideration elsewhere, and all listed authors have approved
the final version.

Esophageal cancer remains one of the most lethal gastrointestinal malignancies worldwide,
with particularly high incidence and mortality in East Asian populations where squamous cell
carcinoma driven by betel nut, tobacco, and alcohol exposure predominates. We conducted a
comprehensive analysis of 2,367 consecutive esophageal cancer cases from a single-center
Taiwan Cancer Registry spanning 2006–2020—one of the largest real-world Asian esophageal
cancer cohorts reported to date. Our key finding is that R0 surgical resection margin
status (HR 0.60, 95% CI 0.53–0.69) and concurrent chemoradiotherapy (HR 0.76, 95% CI
0.67–0.85) are independent prognostic determinants after multivariable adjustment
(C-index 0.705, bootstrap 95% CI 0.694–0.719). Furthermore, unsupervised deep learning
via an autoencoder-UMAP-k-means pipeline identified three biologically distinct patient
clusters—including an adenocarcinoma-enriched subgroup (n=153) and a cervical/upper
esophagus cohort (n=185)—with differential survival trajectories that are invisible to
conventional AJCC staging alone.

We believe this work is well-suited for *Cancers* for several reasons. The journal has
published extensively on gastrointestinal malignancies, multimodal treatment outcomes, and
machine learning applications in oncology. Our manuscript contributes real-world registry
evidence from a population underrepresented in the literature (Taiwanese/East Asian), adds
bootstrap-validated prognostic modelling, and applies accessible deep learning methodology
(PyTorch autoencoder, UMAP, DeepSurv) that can be reproduced by the oncology research
community. The dataset spans 15 years (2006–2020), captures 7 figure panels across all
treatment dimensions, and provides the first deep learning subtype characterization of
esophageal cancer in a Taiwan Cancer Registry cohort.
