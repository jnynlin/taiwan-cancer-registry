🔑 Central Finding
PAM50-Basal patients segregate into two transcriptomically distinct clusters (C5 vs C6) with a 1.8-fold difference in median OS (614 vs 1,093 days). HOXB3, HOXB4, HOXB5, and HOXB6 are the dominant genomic drivers by gene-SHAP analysis, and this signature is confirmed in two external cohorts (METABRIC n=2,000 and SCAN-B/GSE96058 n=3,273) on independent platforms. Overall cluster survival separation replicates strongly in METABRIC (p=1.28×10⁻¹²). Basal-specific OS did not replicate (insufficient power; n=209–325 Basal per external cohort), and cluster assignment adds ΔC=+0.005 over PAM50+stage. Findings are hypothesis-generating; HOXB is nominated as a candidate stratifier for future Basal/TNBC studies.

Known Limitations (in Manuscript)

  • ΔC=+0.005 — clusters do not add survival information beyond PAM50+stage+age; prognostic claim is molecular, not independently prognostic.
  • Basal OS not replicated in external cohorts (insufficient n; GSE96058 heavily censored at 52 months).
  • HOXB5 p=0.289 in TCGA — not significant in training cohort DEG; only HOXB4 (FDR=0.0007) is significant in TCGA. All four genes are significant in METABRIC and GSE96058.
  • Bulk RNA-seq only — no single-cell resolution; HOXB gradient may reflect tumour microenvironment composition.
  • 25.6% gene dropout — METABRIC microarray missing 25.6% of TCGA HVGs; zero-imputed.
