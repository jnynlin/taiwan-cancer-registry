Abstract

Background: Multiple primary cancers (MPC) are an increasingly common clinical problem, yet the patterns by which specific cancers co-occur within a patient — and the shared carcinogenic mechanisms they imply — remain poorly characterised in Asian populations, where betel quid chewing, tobacco smoking, and alcohol consumption create a distinctive mucosal field-cancerization risk. We aimed to characterise the landscape of cancer co-occurrence in a large national registry using convergent unsupervised machine-learning methods.

Methods: We analysed 78,578 patients (84,157 primary cancers) from the Taiwan Cancer Registry (2006–2020). Three independent unsupervised methods were applied to a binary patient × cancer-site matrix: pairwise association rule mining (lift, odds ratio), non-negative matrix factorisation (NMF, k = 7), and a deep binary-cross-entropy autoencoder followed by UMAP and k-means clustering (k = 3). Concordance across methods was assessed. Standardised incidence ratios (SIR) with Poisson exact confidence intervals (288,424 person-years, FDR-corrected) quantified second-primary risk, and directional trajectory mining separated synchronous from metachronous second primaries.

Results: 4,068 patients (5.2%) developed ≥ 2 distinct primary cancers. Sixty-nine index → target pairs showed significantly elevated second-primary risk (FDR < 0.05); the highest were esophagus → larynx (SIR 12.2, 95% CI 5.6–23.2) and esophagus → hypopharynx (SIR 12.0, 8.1–17.3). All three unsupervised methods converged on three co-occurrence domains: (I) an upper aerodigestive squamous-cell-carcinoma (SCC) field — 96% male, median onset 55 years, 68.8% mortality, 13% third-cancer rate; (II) a female reproductive-tract/breast cluster (49.1% mortality); and (III) an age-related visceral/urological cluster. Trajectory mining revealed two temporal modes: upper aerodigestive pairs were bidirectional and synchronous (60% within 6 months), whereas breast and colorectal second primaries were unidirectional and metachronous (10–24% synchronous, FDR < 0.01).

Conclusions: Three orthogonal unsupervised methods independently identified the same upper aerodigestive field-cancerization domain as the dominant driver of MPC in Taiwan. The synchronous, bidirectional presentation of upper aerodigestive pairs supports systematic, simultaneous pan-aerodigestive endoscopic surveillance in any patient presenting with one such SCC.

Association rule mining, NMF (k = 7), and the autoencoder (k = 3) all independently recovered the same three patient clusters. Methodological convergence across three distinct mathematical frameworks is the central strength of this paper.

Methods overview

Association Rule Mining

Support, confidence, lift, OR (Fisher exact) for all ordered cancer pairs. Replicated by sex and age stratum. Lift > 1.5 threshold for enrichment.

NMF (k = 7)

Non-negative matrix factorisation on the multi-primary subset (n = 4,068). Rank selected by silhouette + reconstruction error. Produces 7 interpretable latent programs.

Autoencoder + UMAP + k-Means

Binary BCE autoencoder (64→32→12 dims, 150 epochs). UMAP for visualisation. k-means on full 12-dim latent space (k = 3 by cosine silhouette). Applied to all 78,578 patients.

SIR + Trajectory Mining

Internal-reference SIR (sex × age strata, 288,424 person-years). Jensen 2014 directional trajectory method. Synchronous = ≤ 6 months. FDR correction (Benjamini–Hochberg) throughout.

Novelty context

A systematic literature search confirmed no prior paper applies NMF or deep autoencoder clustering to a national cancer registry to extract pan-cancer co-occurrence domains in an Asian population. Existing work uses single-index-site SIR analyses (e.g. Huang et al. 2009, BMC Cancer) or pairwise transition matrices. Our contribution is:

  • Methodological convergence — three independent algorithms, same result
  • Pan-registry scope — all 46 sites simultaneously, not single-index analyses
  • Temporal modes — synchronous/metachronous distinction with trajectory direction testing
  • Taiwan-specific — betel quid + tobacco + alcohol synergy captured at population scale
  • Actionable clinical output — systematic panendoscopy recommendation grounded in SIR + timing data

Closest existing literature: Huang et al. 2009 (BMC Cancer) — SIR for esophageal second primary in pharyngeal patients, same Taiwan registry, earlier cohort (does not overlap 2006–2020). Li et al. 2001 (European J Cancer) — Italian registry O/E ratio; no axis decomposition.
