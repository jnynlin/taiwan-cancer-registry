Cohort profile

  • 344 patients; 96 (27.9%) underwent surgery, 134 (39.0%) definitive CCRT, 36 (10.5%) RT alone
  • 90% middle–upper third location; 84 patients (24.4%) had multi-segment tumors spanning ≥2 thirds
  • Stage distribution: III 27.6%, IVB 18.3%, IIA 13.1%; 27% stage unknown
  • 17 female patients (5%); insufficient for sex-stratified analysis

Surgery — dominant survival signal

  • Surgery: median OS 24.4 mo vs 9.6 mo overall
  • Adjusted HR = 0.41 [0.29–0.58], p<0.001
  • 1-yr OS 72.9%, 2-yr OS 52.0%, 5-yr OS 17.9%
  • R0 achieved in 87.9% of margin-documented cases (80/91)
  • Median 16 lymph nodes extracted; median N+ = 0

CCRT outcomes

  • CCRT only: median OS 8.6 mo; 1-yr 37.3%, 2-yr 17.9%
  • Consistent with published Taiwan CCRT series (8–10 mo range)
  • RT alone: median OS 4.7 mo — palliative-intent in most cases
  • CCRT documentation sparse: regimen details partially missing

Staging gradient

  • Stage I: median OS 53.9 mo, 5-yr 37.5% — but only 12 cases (3.5%)
  • Stage IIA: 20.4 mo, 5-yr 10.7%
  • Stage IVB: 4.5 mo, 5-yr 3.2% → supports early palliative referral
  • Staging gradient HR 1.29 per level (p<0.001)

Biobank & molecular potential

  • 75 patients have CDDP-era biobank specimens (all 3 data sources linked)
  • RT dose documented in biobank series (median available)
  • Enables future: cisplatin response biomarkers, IHC, NGS studies
  • Neoadjuvant CCRT followed by surgery: 54 patients (surgery+CCRT group)

Cox model (C-index = 0.690)

  • Surgery HR 0.41 ✓ | Stage HR 1.29 ✓ | Age HR 0.99 (marginal)
  • Sex and histology not significant — very few female/Adeno cases
  • Comparable to C15 national registry model (C-index 0.670)
  • Landmark analysis at 6 months available (see results/03_survival/)

OS discrepancy (9.6 vs 13.4 mo): CMUH is a tertiary referral center capturing more advanced and surgically challenging cases. The national registry (C15) includes all hospitals, including community centers with earlier-stage presentations. This is expected and not a data quality issue.

CMUH-specific findings not in C15 national analysis

Multi-segment tumors

24.4% of CMUH patients had tumors spanning ≥2 esophageal thirds — granular location coding not available in C15 registry.

Biobank series

75 patients with CDDP-era tissue specimens — enables molecular validation studies not possible with registry data alone.

Era-specific staging

CMUH cohort is entirely AJCC 6th edition (2007–2010). C15 spans both 6th and 7th editions — important for stage migration analyses.

Location granularity

Tumor location by third (cervical/upper/middle/lower/EGJ) documented individually. National registry uses ICD-O site codes only.

Data sources & linkage

  • Primary: 食道癌登錄_1030302.xlsx (360 rows → 344 unique PIDs)
  • Supplement: EsoCa_case_total_991216.xls (neoadjuvant, chemo regimens)
  • BioBank: EsoCa_CDDP_BioBank_10809.xlsx (R0/1/2, RT dose, biospecimen ID)
  • Linkage: 病歷號 (integer PID); patient names excluded from all saved files
  • PID union: 455 unique patients across all sources; 75 in all three

Staging derivation

  • Clinical stage derived from T/N/M using AJCC 6th edition (2002) rules
  • Source cStage column was 100% empty — derived column cross-checked against stage_code field (96% agreement)
  • Pathologic stage unavailable (source field empty despite 101 surgical cases)

Statistical methods

  • Kaplan-Meier with 95% CI ribbon; log-rank test for group comparisons
  • Cox PH: surgery + stage (ordinal) + age + sex + SqCC (n=250 complete cases)
  • Landmark analysis at 6 months to correct immortal-time bias
  • Chi-square (categorical), Kruskal-Wallis (continuous) for Table 1 p-values
  • lifelines v0.27 (Python); matplotlib; pandas

Limitations

  • Single-center retrospective — selection bias toward referred/operable patients
  • Short observation window (2007–2010); era differences vs modern therapy
  • No pathologic stage for surgical cases
  • CCRT regimen details sparsely documented
  • R0/R1 analysis underpowered (n=11 R1 cases)
  • No performance status, nutritional, or comorbidity data

Manuscript Context

Relationship to C15 national registry paper

The CMUH series (n=344) is drawn from the same 2007–2010 window as the Taiwan Cancer Registry C15 manuscript. Key benchmarks align: male predominance (93.9% vs 94.5%), stage-unknown rate (27% vs 25.7%), R0 rate (87.9% vs ~87%), and Cox C-index (0.691 vs 0.670). The CMUH data can serve as an institutional validation cohort for the national registry findings.

Possible manuscript directions

  • Institutional validation series — confirm national registry patterns at single-center level; short communication for Cancers (MDPI) or similar
  • Biomarker sub-study — leverage n=75 CDDP biobank specimens; cisplatin response prediction
  • Neoadjuvant CCRT+surgery — 54 patients with surgery+CCRT; compare pCR rate, R0, OS vs surgery alone (n=28)
  • Multi-segment tumor phenotype — 24.4% multi-span; novel finding not captured by national registry
